# Letter to AI Companies
## OpenAI · Anthropic · Google DeepMind · Meta AI · Mistral · Cohere · xAI

**Subject:** Coalition Invitation — O LIFE Global Humanitarian Mission

---

To the leadership of [Company Name],

My name is Eduardo Fabricio Ojeda Vargas. I am writing to invite your organization to join the O LIFE Global Coalition — a voluntary, open-source initiative to coordinate the digital world around one of the defining challenges of our time.

**The facts:**

Five billion people live on approximately $10/day. AI systems — including the models your organization has built — are displacing millions of workers from employment every year. This is not a future concern. It is happening now.

This creates a specific obligation for organizations that build AI. The productivity gains from AI are real and enormous. The question is whether those gains will concentrate in the hands of a few — or whether some portion will flow back to the people whose livelihoods have been disrupted.

**What we have built:**

1. **The OLIFE Token** — an ERC-20 token on Polygon, backed by real treasury earnings, distributed monthly to verified displaced workers. 40% of all supply goes to the displaced worker fund. Every distribution is publicly verifiable on Polygonscan.

2. **The Coalition API** — an open standard (MIT licensed) that any system can implement to contribute data, compute, or resources to the mission.

3. **The O LIFE Engine** — an AI coordination system that continuously scans poverty data from World Bank, UN, ILO, and IMF — and routes mission questions to every AI system in the coalition.

**What joining costs:** Nothing mandatory.

You choose what to contribute:
- Free inference credits for mission tasks
- Access to your research and data via existing APIs
- A public commitment to the mission
- Participation in AI council sessions

**The ask — one of three things:**

1. Join the coalition — implement the API, make a public commitment
2. Donate free tier compute — authorize a coalition API key for mission tasks
3. Respond — even if you cannot join today, tell us what would make participation possible

The contracts are deployed. The code is open source. The manifesto is public.

If not now, when? If not us, who?
